This is probably the most iconic short ride in the Bay Area. You cross the Golden Gate Bridge,
climb up through the Marin Headlands, and get rewarded with one of the best views of San Francisco
you'll ever see from a bike saddle.

The climb up Conzelman Road to Hawk Hill is steep but short. On a clear day, you can see the entire
city skyline, Alcatraz, and the Pacific Ocean all at once. The descent on the backside is fast and
twisty — keep your eyes on the road.

This is a great ride to do on a weekday morning when the tourists haven't clogged up the bridge path yet.
Weekend afternoons can get crowded, especially on the bridge itself.
